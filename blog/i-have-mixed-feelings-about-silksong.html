I Have Mixed Feelings About Silksong

October 6, 2025

In case you somehow missed it, Hollow Knight: Silksong, the ridiculously anticipated follow up to the original Hollow Knight, released just over a month ago. I, like many people, waited with excited for this game to finally release for 6 years. The original Hollow Knight is quite possibly my favourite game I've ever played, so I was looking forward to seeing what else Team Cherry had cooking - though I was admittedly worried it wouldn't live up to the hype.

Now that it's out and I've finished the secret ending, I can say... I think it did?

Look, I know that's really uncertain sounding, it's just... a little weird. It's a good game. It's a really good game. But it also has a lot of elements that really bothered me and just made me feel exhausted by the end of the game, and I just didn't quite get as invested in Pharloom as I did with Hallownest. 

Before I go much further, it's worth noting that this is gonna get into spoilers for both Hollow Knight and Silksong. If you care about that kind of thing and haven't finished the games, you should probably stop reading!

To start things off... the introduction to the game is fantastic. Moss Grotto is gorgeous, the Moss Mother fight was a fun battle to get back into the swing of new Hollow Knight bosses, and there were a lot of interesting mysteries set up. Who are these people? Why did they kidnap Hornet? Why and how did a little white fly break the seals of binding holding Hornet captive?

Proceeding through the rest of the world, I think I just wasn't quite as invested because I wasn't wandering around wondering what happened here. I was focused on my goal of getting to the Citadel and getting some answers about why I was kidnapped. Which is fine! This isn't Hollow Knight, there's going to be differences. Honestly, that's how I feel about a lot of these elements - they aren't necessarily bad, they just resulted in a game I didn't quite like as much as the original.
